# 3. Active Cycle

## 3.1 The Facility Floor

The play area is divided into three columns:

| Column | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Centre | Storage ring + Prep ring + Beamline hutches | Main play area |
| Right (180px) | Active Jobs & Rapid Review panel — live status of all in-flight and pending proposals | Read-only HUD, not a physical room |

A wall with a single door gap separates the **Prep Room** from the **Hutch**. The player must pass through the gap to carry samples between rooms. The door gap position is fixed; the player cannot carry samples through the wall.

## 3.2 Sample Workflow — The Core Loop

Each sample in a proposal batch follows this mandatory sequence:

| Step         | Action                                                                           | Location       |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| 1. COLLECT   | Walk to User NPC → [Space] pick up raw sample                                    | Prep Room      |
| 2. DEPOSIT   | Walk to matching lab (Wet Lab 🧪 or Dry Lab 🔩) → [Space] deposit (auto-processes 1.0–2.0s) | Wet/Dry Lab |
| 3. COLLECT   | Walk back to lab → [Space] pick up prepped sample                                | Wet/Dry Lab    |
| 4. ROUTE     | Carry prepped sample to the hutch whose technique matches the proposal           | Corridor       |
| 5. INTERLOCK | Stand inside the matching hutch → hold position (1.8s) with prepped sample       | Hutch entrance |
| 6. MEASURE   | Walk to the beamline's Control Room → [Space] start measurement (auto-runs 4.5s) | Control Room   |
| 7. COLLECT   | Walk back to Control Room → [Space] pick up result → sample complete             | Control Room   |

**Steps cannot be skipped.** The player can carry up to 3 samples in different stages simultaneously.

Stations always check for ready **OUTPUT** before accepting new input (pickup priority over deposit).

> **Technique routing (Step 4):** Each beamline is permanently assigned one technique. A prepped sample can only be taken to the hutch whose technique matches its proposal. Approaching the wrong hutch triggers an error message. See [4. Proposal System](4.%20Proposal%20System.md) § 4.3 for beamline assignment details.

## 3.3 Player Inventory

- Player can hold up to **3 samples** simultaneously
- Each held sample shown as a coloured dot above the player, labelled **R** (raw), **P** (prepped), or a lock icon (interlocked)
- Sample colour matches the job slot colour (blue/orange/green/purple/brown)
- Holding 3 samples does **not** prevent depositing at a station — only prevents collecting new samples from User NPCs

## 3.4 Stations

| Station | Room | Interaction | Base Duration | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wet Lab 🧪 | Sample Prep Room (top) | Deposit (Space) → auto-processes biological samples | 1.0–2.0s | 1 (upgradeable to 2) |
| Dry Lab 🔩 | Sample Prep Room (bottom) | Deposit (Space) → auto-processes non-biological samples | 1.0–2.0s | 1 (upgradeable to 2) |
| Experimental Hutch | Hutch (must match proposal technique) | Player-held action (stand still with prepped sample) | 1.8s | 1 per beamline |
| Measurement Console | Control Room | [Space] start → auto-runs | 4.5s | 1 (upgradeable to 2) |

> **Lab type routing (Step 2):** Each proposal has a lab type (wet or dry) determined by the application. Wet samples (biological: proteins, drugs, viruses, tissue, etc.) must go to the Wet Lab 🧪; dry samples (minerals, alloys, semiconductors, etc.) must go to the Dry Lab 🔩. Depositing at the wrong lab triggers an error message.

- A station with capacity > 1 processes multiple samples **in parallel** (not sequentially)
- Shows a progress bar and percentage label while processing
- Green ✅ READY indicator appears when output is waiting
- Amber stripe appears when the station is full

## 3.5 Beamline Users (User NPCs)

Beamline users are the scientists whose experiments the player is running. They stand inside the prep ring handing over raw samples.

- At game start, **1 random committed proposal** becomes active and its NPC appears in the prep ring; the remaining committed proposals appear as **click-to-start cards** in the right-side Active Jobs panel
- The player can run up to **5 concurrent active users** simultaneously — there is no hard cap below this number
- When a queued slot becomes visible, the player clicks the card to bring in that user; **queued proposals do not auto-promote**
- Each active job owns a **stable NPC slot** for the duration it is active; the slot's position is **re-randomised every time a new job is activated**, so each NPC spawns at a fresh location
- Positions are picked randomly from the valid arc of the prep ring, avoiding beamline corridors; radius is also randomised within the ring band
- Interaction ring glows bright when player is within pickup range
- NPC disappears once **all samples in its batch are fully processed** (not just collected)
- Queued proposals have no NPC until activated via the right-side panel (blue border, 📋 icon)

### 3.5.1 User Leave Timer

Each active beamline user has a **leave timer** shown as a small progress bar above their head. The timer counts down from the moment the NPC enters the facility and runs continuously until the job completes or time expires — it does not pause once samples are collected.

**Duration formula:** `sum of (prep + setup + meas) for all samples × 2.5` — roughly **22.5s per sample** (e.g. ~67s for 3 samples, ~112s for 5).

If the timer expires before the job is fully processed:
- The user leaves — any uncollected samples **and any samples currently held by the player** are lost
- Samples already in prep or measurement continue processing normally
- Rep penalty: proportional to the total fraction lost, including held samples (`lost/total × rep × 0.5`, min 1)
- If the lost held samples were the last ones with no in-flight work remaining, the job completes immediately with the rep penalty already applied

The bar shifts green → orange (50%) → red (75%) as urgency increases. Queued users (not yet in an active slot) do not have a visible timer — it starts when their slot opens.

## 3.6 Active Jobs Panel (Right HUD)

Shows the proposal hub — always stocked with **5 visible slots**. Each slot shows one of:

| Slot State | Border | Icon | Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active job (in progress) | teal | colour dot | Read-only status display |
| Committed queue (click to start) | blue | 📋 | Click to bring in the user NPC |
| Rapid Review proposal | amber | ⚡ | Click to accept; bonus proposal, no penalty |
| Empty | grey | — | Filled automatically by hub restock |

**Per active-job slot displays:**
- Job name, technique (colour-coded by beamline)
- Samples done / total
- Current status (`N waiting · M prepping · K measuring` or `→ collect sample`)
- Pip progress bar coloured by job slot index

**Proposal Hub Restock:** Whenever the sum of active + queued + pending proposals drops below 5, a new random Rapid Review proposal is instantly generated to keep all 5 slots filled. The player always has choices visible — empty slots should never appear during normal gameplay.

A `+N queued` label above the panel shows how many committed proposals are still waiting for a slot.

## 3.7 Rapid Review Proposals

- Bonus proposals that fill empty panel slots during the active cycle
- Displayed with a ⚡ icon and amber card border
- **No commitment penalty** regardless of completion
- Expire after **40 seconds** if not accepted — when they expire, the hub restock generates a replacement immediately
- Rapid proposals are auto-generated by the hub restock system; the first may appear before the cycle timer starts if too few committed proposals were selected

## 3.8 Postdoc NPCs

Postdocs are helper NPCs purchased via the upgrade shop (max 2). Each postdoc is assigned a **unique name** from a pool of 23 when hired, shown as a 3-letter badge on their sprite. They move at the same speed as the player and are blocked by the storage ring.

Navigation uses proactive ring-avoidance: when the straight-line path to a target crosses the inner ring, the postdoc orbits tangentially around it rather than trying to push through. A 2-second stuck-safety valve forces a re-evaluation if the postdoc cannot make progress.

**Level 1 AI (current):** Postdocs wander randomly when idle. When the player enters a room and starts an action (deposits a sample, begins experiment setup, or starts a measurement), the nearest unassigned postdoc walks to that room. Once it arrives, it "takes over" — the action continues ticking even after the player leaves the room. The postdoc cannot initiate actions on its own.

See [6. Progression](6.%20Progression.md) § 6.4 for the full intelligence level breakdown.
